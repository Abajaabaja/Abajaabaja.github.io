Seven Wonders of the World

The Roman Colosseum

One of the original 7 Wonders of the Ancient World, the Roman Colosseum is still the largest amphitheater in the world, holding an estimated audience of between 50,000 and 80,000 spectators at once. It represents one of the greatest architectural and engineering feats ever accomplished by mankind, and was built in a span of just 10 years, reaching completion in 80 A.D. Partially ruined by natural disasters and stone robbers, it remains one of the most iconic symbols of Rome and of Italy.

  • Height: 48 meters
  • Location: Piazza del Colosseo, 1, Roma, Italy
  • It is the largest surviving amphitheatre in the world. Built during the time between 70 AD to 72 AD by the famous Emperor Vespasian.
