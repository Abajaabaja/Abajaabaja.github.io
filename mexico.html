Chichen Itza

Originally built by the Maya civilization as early as 600 AD, Chichen Itza was an important ancient city featuring several famous pyramids and other architectural styles. The ruins of the city are federal property and the invaluable cultural site is maintained by Mexico’s National Institute of Anthropology and History, hosting well over 1 million tourists per year. The renowned structure of Mayan ruins, the city of Chichen Itza boasts of a World Heritage Site of a massive temple – El Castillo, built in the shape of a pyramid. Dedicated to the God of Kukulkan.

  • Height: 30 meters
  • Location: Yucatan, Mexico
  • It is consists of 91 steps on each side which when totalled with the final step of the temple, equals the number of days in one Haab year.
